DESIGN

CHALLENGE

Three questions were posed at the beginning of this project:

How can a digital solution make cultural heritage experiences in Sweden more visible and accessible?

How can a digital solution promote engagement, motivation, and entertainment for cultural heritage experiences?

How can a digital solution promote pedagogy and learning in cultural heritage experiences in Sweden?

GOAL

Increase accessibility and visibility

Ensure cultural heritage sites were easy to discover and navigate, both remotely and on-site.

Address challenges related to the lack of information and difficulty accessing these experiences.

Promote engagement and motivation

Foster meaningful connections with cultural heritage through interactive and entertaining features.

Encourage active participation and sustained interest in exploring heritage sites.

Support learning and pedagogy

Provide educational content that enriched users’ understanding of the history and significance of cultural sites.

Use innovative tools, such as gamification and contextual storytelling, to facilitate immersive learning experiences.

MY ROLE

In this project, I played a comprehensive role in the ideation, development, and evaluation of a digital solution aimed at enhancing cultural heritage experiences by promoting accessibility, engagement, and learning.

Key responsibilities included:

Ideation and conceptualization: Developed the core concept of a gamified exploration and mystery-solving experience that combined augmented reality (AR) with on-site interactions. This included designing scenarios where users could solve mysteries tied to the history of real locations, fostering both engagement and education.

Gamification development: Designed and implemented the gamified system, integrating interactive elements and AR technology to create an immersive and engaging experience for users.

User research and testing: Conducted surveys and user research to gather insights into user needs, preferences, and behaviors. Facilitated user testing sessions to evaluate the prototype and gather feedback for iterative improvements.

Data analysis: Analyzed qualitative and quantitative data from surveys and testing sessions to refine the design and ensure the solution effectively met user expectations and project goals.

TESTING

The testing phase of the project was conducted in multiple stages to ensure a deep understanding of user needs and refine the solution accordingly.

1. Quantitative study

The initial phase involved a quantitative study with over 60 participants from the target audience. This large-scale survey provided valuable insights into user behaviors, preferences, and challenges related to cultural heritage experiences.

2. Qualitative in-depth interviews

Based on the findings from the quantitative study, the scope was narrowed down for a more detailed exploration. In this phase, qualitative in-depth interviews were conducted with a select group of participants. These interviews provided nuanced understanding and personal perspectives that complemented the initial survey data.

3. Needs analysis and development of personas

A comprehensive needs analysis was conducted using the combined findings from the quantitative and qualitative research. This analysis helped identify key user pain points, desires, and motivations.

From this analysis, personas were created to represent the core user groups. These personas acted as a guiding tool throughout the design process, ensuring that the solution addressed the specific needs and expectations of the target audience.
